What we eat is involved in many processes in our body and brain, and I think it is important to know that, although when we think of a good recipe the first things that come to mind are its taste, smell, texture, and appearance, where food really has an impact is also in our brain. Although, of course, we all know that we don't always feel like eating everything or like everything, and eating can become more complicated.

Lately, in the health world, there have been many advances and studies investigating the effects that nutrition can have beyond our metabolism or physical health. Food psychology, neurogastronomy, and gastrophysics have been demonstrating that what we eat has a direct effect on our mood, our memories, and our decisions, since eating, in the end, is a constant conversation between our gut and the nervous system.

For this reason, I wanted to make a compilation of recipes from this platform that, based on these studies, can help us in this regard—specifically in what way, and what are the foods that make these recipes useful.

Let's start with the [MACARRONS DE SALMÓ](https://jorbites.com/recipes/67ed4ded8b942e6021b2da96) recipe, which would be an ALLY AGAINST STRESS. This is because specific nutrients, such as Omega-3 fatty acids, complex carbohydrates, and antioxidants, play a crucial role in regulating cortisol (the stress hormone) and in synthesizing neurotransmitters like serotonin, known as the happiness hormone. Therefore, the regular consumption of oily fish and quality carbohydrates significantly improves the biomarkers associated with emotional well-being. This makes this recipe ideal for a stressful day when you need to regulate your nervous system and "start from scratch," since the salmon provides us with Omega-3.

In addition, macaroni pasta generates a progressive absorption of tryptophan, which is also the chemical precursor of serenity.

The second recipe would be [TOMBET DE LLAMPUGA](https://jorbites.com/recipes/6917246efb90f48ee38b773f) or [SAMFAINA AMB BACALLÀ](https://jorbites.com/recipes/6919c44efef661740f22d10e), which would be an ALLY TO BOOST APPETITE. This is because our brain creates a "taste expectation" based exclusively on the color and geometric arrangement of food, which makes us associate recipes with bright and varied colors with nutrient richness and freshness. What this ultimately triggers is the activation of the dopaminergic reward system, making us feel excited and more satisfied before the first taste. This makes the color contrast of eggplants, red and green bell peppers, zucchini, and ripe tomatoes send a direct signal to the brain that it is about to receive a revitalizing meal.

The third recipe would be [BOTIFARRA AMB MONGETES BLANQUES](https://jorbites.com/recipes/6910be233016128c9135033a) or, failing that, [MANDONGUILLES AMB SÈPIA](https://jorbites.com/recipes/6910be233016128c9135033a), which would be our ALLIES TO EVOKE MEMORIES THAT MAKE US FORGET THE FEELING OF LONELINESS. This is because, in the end, senses like taste, smell, and hearing can transport us to lived moments and sensations, even making us cry or laugh with joy, as the olfactory system and taste stimuli are connected directly to the amygdala and the hippocampus (the brain regions responsible for processing emotions and long-term memory), which can evoke memories associated with the safety of home. This makes traditional dishes perfect for this ally, since they end up being comfort recipes—though, of course, they won't be the same for everyone.

And the last recipe would be [GELAT DE COCO](https://jorbites.com/recipes/687f539b64c930d28e66c936), which could be our PERFECT ALLY TO PREVENT ANXIETY, because sometimes constantly depriving ourselves of food and the pleasant stimuli it brings can be counterproductive, as it would increase levels of the hormone ghrelin (which causes food cravings) and lead to emotional impulsivity eating. Studies have shown that eating desserts or foods that bring us pleasure with mindfulness increases psychological satiety, thus reducing impulsive intake and improving our relationship with food.

Therefore, I would say that for the next time you enter the kitchen and look for a Jorbites recipe, it would be good to keep these factors and your mood at that moment in mind. Ask yourself what you need, and then indeed, get cooking! Enjoy your meal and good mental health!
